Karelian Bear Dog

Karelian Bear Dogs are eager hunters and independent. They have sharp sense of smell and very developed for hunting game. They can be territorial towards other male dogs, however, they are typically never aggressive towards people (instead they might be more reserved). These dogs are typically silent hunters that only bark once the game is stopped or treed.

History

The Karelian Bear Dog is an ancient dog breed that shares ancestry with the Komi dog, however, this causes difficulty in figuring out the details in the specifics of this dog breed's origin. There is some suggestion that they may have originated in Northwestern Europe and was used by Finnish and Russian farmers and hunters due to the hunting skills.

Maintenance

Take care to notice that even though these dogs can be affectionate and loyal to the owner, they are considered wilder than domesticated so it is important to start socialization at young ages due to the hunting aspect of the dog's personality. They require daily exercise and a job. At least one hour of vigorous daily walks leashed will not be enough. Their coats require minimal grooming of once or twice a week to remove loose fur and matting. Trim their nails every three to four weeks as needed.
