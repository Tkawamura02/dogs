Hokkaido

Hokkaido's are medium sized dogs that have thicker coats than a lot of the other Japanese breed dogs. They come in many colors and are very loyal and intelligent. Hokkaido's are commonly associated with loyalty and dedication since they are very adept on pleasing their human families.

History

There is some debate on this, however, the generally accepted history is that this breed was migrated with the Ainu people from Honshu to Hokkaido during the 1140s. These dogs back then were known as "bear dogs" due to their fierce and brave hunting, however, when the need for bear-hunting dogs declined, the breed became watchdogs and family companions.

Maintenance

They typically require regular grooming due to their thick and long coats. At least twice a week to prevent their fur or coat from matting or allowing it to build up. They undergo two shedding periods during each year which will require more coat care. These dogs are very intelligent making training much easier. They require exercise time and space and will be happy if regular open and free exercise is gien to them (ie. camping trips or hiking sessions).
