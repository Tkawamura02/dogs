Cane Corso

Cane Corsos ar intelligent and independent by nature. They are extremely active and alert. It is very important to properly socialize this breed and teach or train basic skills to be successful as adults. This is NOT an ideal fit for a first time dog owner! Unfortunately, a big proportion of abandoned Cane Corso are simply because families and owners were unaware of how to train their dog.

History

Cane Corso (pronounced Kah-nay Corso) dogs are thought to have been descended from the Greek Molossus dogs after being bred with England fighting dogs. They were thouhg to have been used for attacking wild animals and hunted alongside the Romans. They almost went extinct after World War II but ressurged in Italy in the 1970s and brought to the United States in the 1980s. Their name stands for "guard of the estate" in Latin.

Maintenance

They are extremely intelligent and require lifelong training so that owner expectations are clear. If there's no work or proper training, then they will act on instinct. Socialization must start as a young age and throughout their lives. Introduce them to as many situations and noises in a training manner. Because of their intelligence, they require mental stimulation and regular exercise. These dogs are happiest when they have clear and regular tasks without these, they can resort to destructive behavior. They have short thick coats that shed heavily twice a year, so brush with soft-bristle brushed twice a week!
