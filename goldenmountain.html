Golden Mountain Dog

Golden Mountain Dogs are a mixed breed between Golden Retriever and Bernese Mountain dogs which is why these dogs seem to inherit a significant amount of traits from both breeds. They make excellent family dogs and get along with pets and children. It is imoprtant to note that they prefer colder climates so these are not apartment dogs.

History

There is not a lot of information about how the Golden Mountain Dog evolved but Golden Retrievers (which is one of the parent dogs) was bred into existence during the mid-1800s by a nobleman named Lord Tweedmouth in Scotland. The other parent dog, Bernese Mountain Dog, was originated from cross breeding the Roman Mastiff type dogs with Swiss Alps farm dogs and have been used on farms in the Alps for over 2,000 years.

Maintenance

They require a lot of maintenance. Their thick long coat seems to be in constant state of shedding although there is some respite through the winter months. They require daily brushing and the house will require frequent vacuuming. Make sure to check their ears as they tend to get inflammation and infection in this are. Wipe their ears with a soft damp cloth and never insert anything into the ear canal. Regularly brush their teeth and regularly trim their nails.
