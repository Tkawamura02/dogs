Dogs

Dogs descend from a species of wolf and have different appearances due to the several hundred breeds available in the world today. Domestic dogs came to be as a result of being kept as pets. They are one of the most popular pet choice on the planet.

Even though the most common belief is that all dogs descend from all wolves, this is not necessarily true. Recent studies suggest that Eurasian wolves are the ancestors to modern dogs but this is a very rocky subject as it requires more research and experimentation.

The story of how dogs came to be is also another gray area that requires more evidence but we do know that genetics suggest that dogs broke off from wild ancestors between 15,000 and 30,000 years ago. Based on other species and evolution, it is believed that the evolution of dogs occured gradually and slowly. It is possible that wolves may have been welcomed into the human home and eventually bred to the many dogs we have now.

Pets back then were most likely used for different purposes like herding or warning. Even though the modern jobs for dogs are not job-heavy. There are some things that dogs do that assist human jobs. A great example is the fact that dog's have a great sense of smell and are able to assist law enforcement by sniffing out drugs and explosives. They are not only using their noses to detect weapons but also life threatening signs like low blood pressure or high blood pressure. Some dogs are well trained in warning their owners when their blood pressure changes too rapidly. They are also used as the most popular service animal and can guide people with disabilities or even people with anxiety.
