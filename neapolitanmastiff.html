Neapolitan Mastiff

Neapolitan Mastiffs are large, powerful dogs known for their protective and loyal nature. They have a distinctive wrinkled and droopy face and come in various coat colors such as black, blue, and mahogany. Neapolitan Mastiffs are known for being calm and gentle with their family but are often wary of strangers.

History

Neapolitan Mastiffs have a rich history that can be traced back to ancient Rome. They were originally used as war dogs and guard dogs, and their intimidating appearance and protective nature made them valued in battle. Over time, Neapolitan Mastiffs became more commonly used for guarding estates and livestock. Today, they are known for their loyalty and protective instincts.

Maintenance

Neapolitan Mastiffs have a short, dense coat that requires regular brushing to keep it clean and free of loose hairs. Their facial wrinkles should be cleaned regularly to prevent skin infections. Daily exercise, such as walks or playtime, is important to keep them physically and mentally stimulated. Neapolitan Mastiffs thrive in a calm and stable environment with consistent training and socialization.
