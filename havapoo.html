Havapoo

Havapoo are large, immensely strong worker famous for a dense coat. Dependable and faithful Swissies earned their feed as herders, drafters, and all-round pasture dogs. They are strong enough to pull heavy carts and agile enough to corral cattle on muontainous farms. They are great for family pets and do best where they can exercise.

History

There is a lot of legends associated with their history, however, the most popular tale states that they descended from the Mollasian, large mastiff-type of dog that once accompanied the Roman Legions on their invasion of the Alps in the first century. Another possibility is that the Roman dogs were bred with a large breed that was indigenous to central Europe during the Neolithic period. They tend to work in remote mountain passes where they haul meat and dairy in dog carts. They are used as watchdogs and family companions.

Maintenance

They have a short coat that is easy to care for with regular brushing and occasional bathing especially during the spring and fall where they have seasonal shedding. Regularly brush their teeth and trim their nails. With their energy, this breed is better suited as a hiking companion than a marathon training partner. This means that they will not be happy with a neighborhood run.
