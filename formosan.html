Formosan Mountain Dog

Formosan Mountain Dogs are also known as Taiwan Dogs which are extremely faithful to their master, keen in sense, alert in movement, bold, and fearless. They are medium-sized dogs with triangular heads, almond shaped eyes, thin pricked ears, and sickle tail. They are not officially a breed but rather live in the streets of Taipei.

History

They genetically can be traced back to 10,000 and 20,000 years ago making them one of the oldest and most primitive dog breeds in the world and are descendants of the South Asian hunting dogs indigenous to the central mountainous regions of Taiwan and was the loyal companion of the ancient hunter in the wild forest. This breed has been an integral part of Taiwan's historical and cultural landscape for thousands of years now and are very popular across the island as a watch and companion dog but are rare and kept for conservation efforts in Taiwan.

Maintenance

They require occassional care becuase of their two-layer coat which requires minimal grooming and occasional bathing. Dental care is recommended as tartar can build up and their nails should be trimmed regularly. Make sure to check their ears regularly to avoid buildup of wax and debris which can result in infection. They require exercise and training as they are very energetic naturally protective.
