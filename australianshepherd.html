Australian Shepherd

Australian Shepherd is known for being smart and loyal but they are also very energetic and active. This level of activity needs to be kept to keep them occupied. They are excellent at dog sports such as herding, tracking, agility, tricks, and obedience. They require a family that is willing to provide structure, training and pletny of exercise. They will herd everything: animals, people, and objects by nipping at them and barking. They can be obnoxious and destructive if left alone without a job to do.

History

Despite their name, they did not originate from Australia. The Basques (France and Spain) relied on Pyrenean Shepherds to work alongside their herds. Their Pyrenean Shepherds crossbread with other working dogs, including Collies and Border Collies, creating the Australian Shepherd. The Basques later immigrated to the United States and their herding dogs were popular for their skill and stamina.

Maintenance

This breed is not known for becoming overweight and have waterproof double coats that require regular grooming. Brush them at least once per week to remove cast-off hair and during spring and fall when these dogs blow their coats with an undercoat rake. Regular nail trims and ear cleanings should also be part of their grooming. These dogs prefer to be on the move so these dogs (if not herding) require lots of exercise.
